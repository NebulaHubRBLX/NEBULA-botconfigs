Linked Roles Verification

This page explains how Nebula uses Discord's Linked Roles verification feature to grant special roles on your server based on your status in other services or communities.

What is Linked Roles?

Linked Roles is a Discord feature that allows bots like ours to securely verify if you meet certain criteria (e.g., being a subscriber on Patreon, having a specific role in another server, or owning a particular item) and then grant you a corresponding role on the server where the bot is installed.

How We Use It

If a server administrator has configured a Linked Role with our bot:

  1. You will click a "Verify" button provided by Discord.
  2. Discord will ask for your permission to share specific, limited metadata with our bot.
  3. Our bot will use this metadata to check if you meet the requirements set by the server admin.
  4. If you meet the requirements, the bot will instruct Discord to grant you the role. If not, it will not.

Data We Request and Why

We only request the minimum metadata necessary to perform the verification. This is typically limited to:

  • Your User ID: To know who to grant the role to.
  • Verification Metadata: The specific piece of information needed to confirm your status (e.g., your subscriber tier, your roles in another server).

We do not request access to your full profile, message history, or any other unnecessary data. All data is processed securely and is not stored permanently unless required for the verification function itself.

Managing Your Linked Roles

You can manage and revoke Linked Role connections at any time through your Discord User Settings ("Connections"). If you revoke a connection, you may lose the associated roles on servers that were granted through it.

Questions?

If you have concerns about how Linked Roles is being used on a specific server, please contact that server's administrators. For technical questions about our implementation, please contact us at soulisyummy@gmail.com.

Interactions Endpoint

This page provides information about our Discord Interactions Endpoint, which is required for the bot to function.

What is an Interactions Endpoint?

An Interactions Endpoint is a secure URL that Discord sends real-time events to, such as slash command invocations, button clicks, and other user interactions. This is the modern, secure way for bots to receive information from Discord, replacing the need for "message content" intent for many features.

Our Endpoint Information

Endpoint URL: no endpoint url yet

Public Key: (Available upon request to Discord for verification)

How It Works

  1. When you use a slash command or interact with a component from our bot, Discord's servers send a secure, signed JSON payload to our endpoint.
  2. Our server receives this payload and verifies its signature using our public key to ensure it's authentic and from Discord.
  3. Our bot processes the interaction and sends a response back through Discord's API.

Privacy and Security

This communication is a secure, server-to-server connection. It does not route through your browser or client. The data sent is limited to the specific interaction event and is processed immediately. We adhere to the data handling principles outlined in our Privacy Policy.

Verification

Discord requires bot developers to verify their endpoint ownership. This ensures that the bot is the legitimate recipient of its interactions. Our endpoint is verified and secure.
